Approach

How I Tell
a Story

Let me show you how I write your stories.

01

Structure First

Every story has a skeleton of tension that determines whether audiences stay or scroll. I engineer that skeleton before writing a single scene. Structure is not a constraint — it is the difference between a story and a sequence of events.

02

The Art of Tension

Tension is not conflict. It is the space between what the audience knows and what they suspect. My scripts are built in that space — calibrated to create dread, wonder, and the desperate need to know what happens next.

03

Pacing as Rhythm

Retention is rhythm. Too slow, audiences drift. Too fast, nothing lands. I write with an internal metronome — knowing when to accelerate, when to let silence breathe, and when to drop the revelation that changes everything.

04

Audience Psychology

I write for the gut first, the mind second. Emotional investment precedes intellectual engagement — and my narratives are designed to hook both, in exactly that order. The reader feels before they think. That sequence is everything.

FAQ

You probably still have
some questions.

What exactly is a storyteller for brands and what are you not?

A storyteller for brands is someone who translates what you do into narratives that audiences feel — not just read or watch. Where traditional copywriting informs, storytelling connects. I write the kind of content that makes your audience stop, stay, and come back. I'm a storyteller, script writer, content writer, and published author. I'm not a video editor, a graphic designer, a social media manager, or a content mill. I build the story. Everything else is downstream of that.

I have never worked with a writer before. Where do I even start?

Right here, honestly. Send me a message through the contact section and tell me what you are building — even if you are not sure how to describe it yet. I ask the right questions, so you don't have to have all the answers before reaching out. We'll figure out the scope, the format, and the best place to begin together.

Do you write in my voice or yours?

Yours always. My job is to disappear into your brand and write so naturally in your voice that your audience never suspects someone else was behind it.

What if I don't like what you write?

That is what revisions are for. Every package includes revision rounds, and I take feedback seriously — not personally. If something is not landing the way you imagined, tell me exactly what feels off and I will fix it.

Can I commission you for a one-off project, or do I need to commit to a package?

One-off projects are absolutely welcome. Not everyone needs a retainer — sometimes you just need one great script or one page of copy done properly. Start with whatever you need right now. If we work well together, the rest tends to follow naturally.

What payment methods do you accept?

PayPal, Payoneer, and direct bank transfer. All prices are negotiated directly and confirmed in writing before any project commences.

How long does it take to get my script or content delivered?

Turnaround depends on the project. A single script is typically delivered within one to two business days. Larger packages and content writing projects have timelines that are agreed upon before work begins — and confirmed in writing so there are no surprises on either side. If you have a specific deadline, tell me upfront and I will be honest about whether I can meet it.

What do you need from me to get started?

Not as much as you might think. A brief on what you are building, who your audience is, and what you want the content to do — that is usually enough to get us moving. If you are not sure how to put that into words yet, send a message anyway. I ask the right questions to draw out everything I need. You do not have to arrive with a perfect brief for us to start a great project.

Do you require a deposit, and how does payment work?

Payment structure is flexible and depends on what we agree on. Depending on the project, we can work with full payment upfront, a milestone-based structure where payment is tied to deliverables, or full payment upon completion. Whatever we settle on will be confirmed in writing before any work begins, so both of us are clear from the start.
